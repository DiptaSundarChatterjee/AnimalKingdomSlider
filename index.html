MOOSE
Moose are the biggest members of the deer family. They can stand up to 6 ft tall and weigh more than 1,000 lbs.Moose are herbivores that eat leaves, bark, and twigs from trees and shrubs. They eat about 73lbs of food a day in the summer and 34 lbs in winter.

ELEPHANT
Elephants are the biggest land animals on Earth. They have large ears, long trunks, and thick skin.Elephants are among the most intelligent animals on Earth. They have large brains and exhibit a wide range of complex behaviors, including problem-solving, tool use, self-awareness, and emotional capabilities.

TIGER
Tigers are the biggest wild cats in the world. They are three feet tall, weigh about 450 pounds, and have three-foot-long tails.They have muscular bodies, strong forelimbs, large heads, and reddish-orange coats with dark stripes. No two tigers have the same stripes.
